Clear aligners are a type of orthodontic treatment that uses a series of clear, removable plastic trays to straighten teeth and correct other orthodontic issues, such as bite problems and crowding. Clear aligners are an alternative to traditional metal braces and are often preferred for their discreet appearance and convenience.

The clear aligner treatment process typically involves several steps:

  • Consultation and evaluation: A patient meets with a qualified dental professional who evaluates their oral health and determines if clear aligners are a suitable treatment option. The dental professional may use X-rays, photographs, and dental impressions to create a 3D model of the patient's teeth.
  • Treatment planning: The dental professional uses the 3D model to develop a treatment plan that outlines the expected duration of treatment and the number of clear aligners needed. The treatment plan is customized to address the patient's specific orthodontic issues.
  • Clear aligner fabrication: The dental professional sends the treatment plan to a dental laboratory, which fabricates a series of clear aligners. Each aligner is slightly different and is designed to gradually move the teeth into the desired position.
  • Treatment: The patient wears each aligner for a specified period, typically two weeks, before moving on to the next aligner in the series. The aligners should be worn for at least 22 hours a day to be effective. The patient returns to the dental professional periodically for checkups and to receive the next set of aligners.
  • Retention: After completing the clear aligner treatment, the patient wears a retainer to maintain the teeth's new position. The dental professional may recommend either a removable or fixed retainer, depending on the patient's individual needs.

How Do CLEAR ALIGNERS Work?

Clear aligners, also known as clear braces, are an orthodontic treatment that uses a series of clear plastic trays to gradually straighten teeth. They work by applying controlled and gentle pressure to the teeth, which gradually moves them into their desired positions over time.

Here's how clear aligners work:

  • Consultation: The first step is a consultation with an orthodontist or dentist who will examine your teeth and determine whether clear aligners are the right treatment for you. If they are, they will take digital impressions of your teeth and create a 3D model of your mouth.
  • Treatment plan: Using the 3D model of your mouth, your orthodontist or dentist will create a treatment plan that maps out the movement of your teeth throughout the treatment. This will help you understand what to expect during the treatment process.
  • Clear aligner trays: Based on your treatment plan, a series of custom-made clear aligner trays will be created for you. You will receive several sets of aligners, and each set will be slightly different to gradually move your teeth into the desired position.
  • Wearing the aligners: You will wear each set of aligners for about 22 hours a day, only removing them to eat, drink, brush, and floss. As you progress through each set of aligners, you will start to notice your teeth moving into their new positions.
  • Regular check-ups: You will need to visit your orthodontist or dentist regularly to ensure that your treatment is progressing as planned. They may make adjustments to your treatment plan as needed.
  • Completion: Once you have completed your treatment, your orthodontist or dentist may recommend that you wear a retainer to prevent your teeth from shifting back to their original positions.

Overall, clear aligners offer a discreet and convenient alternative to traditional braces for patients with mild to moderate orthodontic issues. It is important to work with a qualified dental professional to determine if clear aligners are the right choice for your individual needs and preferences.
